Clive Staples Lewis

The Journey of a Poet, Academic, Literary Critic, and Lay Theologian

Here's a time line of CS Lewis' life:

  • November 29, 1898 - Born in Belfast, Ireland.
  • 1908 - Lewis' mother, Florence Augusta Hamilton Lewis, dies by cancer.
  • 1911 - Enrolls at Cherboug House and abandons Christian faith.
  • 1914 - Privately tutored by atheist W.T. Kirkpatrick.
  • 1916 - Receives a scholarship to University College, Oxford.
  • 1917 - Enlists in British Army.
  • 1918 - Wounded in Battle of Arras. Discharged from British Army.
  • 1925 - Tutors English Language and Literature at Magdalen College, Oxford.
  • 1926 - Meets friend and colleague J.R.R. Tolkien.
  • 1929 - Lewis' father, Albert Lewis, dies in Belfast. Lewis abandons atheism and converts to theism.
  • 1931 - Converts to Christianity.
  • 1936 - Publishes "The Allegory of Love."
  • 1940 - Begins weekly meetings of the "Inklings."
  • 1941 - Begins war-time broadcast talks on Christianity which is later collected as "Mere Christianity."
  • 1947 - Appears on the cover of "Time" Magazine.
  • 1952 - Publishes "Mere Christianity."
  • 1956 - Marries Joy Davidman Gresham.
  • 1960 - Lewis' wife dies.
  • 1963 - CS Lewis dies at the age of 65.

"I have found a desire within myself that no experience in this world can satisfy; the most probable explanation is that I was made for another world."

-CS Lewis, Mere Christianity
